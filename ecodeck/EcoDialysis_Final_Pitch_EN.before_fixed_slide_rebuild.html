Act 1 · The hidden vulnerability4 of 16
The strategic shift

Water scarcity turns EcoDialysis from sustainability into operational resilience.

Hospitals will need to know which water flows are essential, which are wasted, and which can be reused safely.

Why now (external shocks)

  • Plan Eau 2023 made water sobriety a national priority after two drought summers.
  • Drought + summer restrictions now reach hospital catchments.
  • Water + sewer tariffs trending up; basin agencies tightening.
  • Hospital continuity risk: a closed RO = closed dialysis.
  • Public accountability: nephrology must answer for water flows.

Why dialysis is the right first use case

  • Measurable: feed/permeate/reject meters; sessions are countable.
  • Recurring: 3 sessions/week/patient → ~156 sessions/year.
  • High-grade waste: RO reject is treated, near-potable, predictable.
  • Non-clinical reuse is bounded: clear safety line, easier to authorize.
  • Replicable: standard equipment classes across centers.
One sentence: In a water-constrained France, the riskiest hospital water is the water nobody measures.
Healthcare = ~8% of national GHG (~50 MtCO2e). Adaptation first, mitigation second. Gov Ird TSP HCW
Act 2 · The operating problem5 of 16
The measurement answer

You cannot reuse what you cannot measure.

EcoDialysis instruments the dialysis water chain and converts flow data into liters per session, euros, risk, and reuse potential.

4-point metering architecture

  1. RO feedwater meter
  2. RO permeate meter
  3. RO reject meter
  4. Reused-water outlet meter

Optional layer: tank level, conductivity, temperature, microbiology sampling, pump runtime.
Cross-checks: session counts, machine logs, water bill, sewer bill.

Core formulas (auditable)

  • RO reject = feedwater − permeate
  • L/session = monthly reject ÷ monthly sessions
  • capture rate = reused ÷ RO reject
  • substitution rate = reused ÷ non-clinical demand
  • savings (€) = m³ reused × (water + sewer tariff)
  • Carbon note: water-supply-only, not full pathway carbon.
Reference center (transparent baseline): 100 patients · 3 sessions/week · 156 sessions/yr · 15,600 sessions/yr. Low / Base / High reject per session = 220 / 270 / 320 L → 3,432 / 4,212 / 4,992 m³/yr before capture losses. Capture rate target 60–80% in Phase 1. Per Ope TSP
Act 2 · The operating problem6 of 16
The reuse answer

Reuse is not one solution. It is a site-specific decision.

EcoDialysis separates safe non-clinical reuse from clinically impossible or legally risky reuse.

🟢 Green · first candidates

  • Toilet flushing (highest demand match)
  • Irrigation / green spaces
  • Selected technical cleaning
  • Compatible technical loops

🟠 Orange · site-specific

  • Laundry
  • Boilers / steam
  • Cooling towers
  • Sterilizers
  • Floor cleaning · outdoor washing

🔴 Red · not current EcoDialysis

  • Dialysis-water production feed
  • Patient-contact reuse
  • Spent dialysate reuse
  • Mixed wastewater reuse without advanced validation

Technical quality decision (per site)

Conductivity · salinity · microbiology · storage time · temperature · biofilm risk · backflow risk · proximity & seasonality of non-clinical demand.

Decision logic: if quality + nearby demand + legal route all align → Green. If any one breaks → Orange and ask the playbook. Two never break: no patient contact, no return to dialysis-water production. Ope S26 Gem
Act 2 · The operating problem7 of 16
The legal gate

The new French EICH framework opens a door, but not a shortcut.

The opportunity is real, but every hospital project must pass a local safety, network, monitoring and authorization logic.

Before (current law residual)

  • Fragmented reuse logic, no clear non-potable framework for buildings.
  • Each hospital project went one-off via ARS / prefecture.
  • Standardization for dialysis non-clinical reuse difficult.

New framework — confirmed

  • Décret n°2024-796 · 12 July 2024, in force 1 Sept 2024.
  • Framework for domestic uses of EICH (eaux impropres à la consommation humaine).
  • Health facilities = sensitive establishments.
  • Prefectural authorization generally required, with ARS input.
  • Use limited to non-potable domestic / non-clinical uses.

Required controls (every site)

  • Separated network · anti-backflow protection
  • Signage · emergency shut-off
  • Preventive risk management · monitoring
  • Maintenance protocol · carnet sanitaire
  • Hygiene committee (CLIN) alignment

🇦🇺 Bendigo

~1.7 ML/yr reused at hospital scale.
Lesson: reuse can be integrated into hospital ops.

🇦🇺 Queen Elizabeth

~1.5–2 ML/yr.
Lesson: governance + technical ownership matter.

🇦🇺 Barwon

Technical reuse examples (sterilizer/cooling).

Act 3 · Proof this is real9 of 16
The deeper lesson

The winning route is never generic.

EcoDialysis must choose between five playbooks: measure-only, reduce-first, toilet reuse, technical reuse, design-in.

Decision logic per site

  • Low capex / weak infrastructure → reduce-first (disinfection + RO settings)
  • Toilets nearby + simple route → toilet reuse
  • Boiler / laundry / cooling nearby → technical reuse
  • Renovation / new building → design-in
  • Legal uncertainty high → measure + pre-file only
  • RO outdated → RO upgrade first (357 L vs 548 L precedent)
  • Disinfection waste high → schedule optimization first (ANZSN precedent)

Technology maturity matrix (compact)

  • Ready now (Phase 1): RO reject → toilets · RO high-recovery upgrade · disinfection schedule opt. · dialysate flow opt. · metering & dashboards · leak detection · waste segregation.
  • Promising / site-specific (Phase 2): RO reject → boiler/steam/cooling/sterilization/laundry · heat recovery · centralized acid concentrate.
  • Pilot / not first-wave (Phase 3–4 watchlist): sorbent / portable low-water systems · spent dialysate reuse · mixed wastewater treatment · plastic recycling pilots.

Full matrix with clinical boundary + capex / reg complexity in Appendix D.

Appendix AMethodology
Appendix A · How EcoDialysis calculates impact

Methodology — objective · perimeter · assumptions · sensitivity · uncertainty

  1. Define objective and decision use. Audit-to-decide; not academic.
  2. Define perimeter. Clinical (no), technical (RO + dialysate loop + disinfection), procurement (consumables), waste (DASRI/DAOM).
  3. Collect measured activity data and selected emission/tariff factors.
  4. Calculate baseline and intervention scenarios (Low / Base / High).
  5. Run sensitivity on tariffs, capture rates, operating profiles, downtime.
  6. Run uncertainty framing and list data gaps explicitly.
  7. Publish limits and non-covered dimensions.
Discipline: follows the TSP care-pathway carbon guide. Partial water-only carbon ≠ full pathway carbon. TSP Ird

Core formulas

  • RO reject (m³) = feedwater − permeate
  • L/session = monthly reject ÷ monthly sessions
  • capture rate = reused ÷ RO reject
  • substitution rate = reused ÷ non-clinical demand
  • annual savings (€) = m³ reused × (water tariff + sewer tariff)
  • annual CO₂ avoided (water-supply only) = m³ reused × water-supply EF

  • Full carbon neutrality claim.
