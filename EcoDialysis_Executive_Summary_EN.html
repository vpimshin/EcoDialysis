The hidden water vulnerability of dialysis

Hook A 100-patient dialysis centre quietly discharges ~4,200 m³/yr of high-purity RO reject — water already paid for, treated, and lost. Décret n°2024-796 (in force 1 Sept 2024) now authorises non-clinical reuse via the EICH framework. France can turn dialysis from a hidden water risk into a measurable water-resilience asset.

Proof base

  • UK: Canterbury (800 L/h, £7.5k/yr), Bristol (13,140 m³/yr), Ashford design-in £2.5k.
  • ANZ: ANZSN cluster — >160,000 L & 6.4 MWh/yr saved.
  • Spain: 20-centre mean 476.5 L/session.
  • FR: Saint-Exupéry ~1,128 m³, ~30 k€, 5–6 yr.
  • SFNDT 2026: Qd 500→400 ml/min saves ~24 L dialysate + 60–100 L pretreated/session.

Discipline — what we refuse to do

  • No clinical-contact reuse. No used-dialysate recycling.
  • No "carbon neutral" or "zero impact" claims. Water carbon caveat: ~1.0–1.1 tCO₂e on 4,200 m³ — real but secondary; main case is resilience & OPEX.
  • No vendor lock-in. Neutral metrology, neutral integrator.
