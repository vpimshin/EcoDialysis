<h2>A. Executive Summary</h2>
<div class="card">
<ul>
<li>France has ~51,700 dialysis patients; ~77% are in non-autonomous in-center/UDM activity, concentrating opportunity in hospital infrastructure footprints (EcoDialysis_Perplexity.pdf / .docx).</li>
<li>Session-level benchmark used in the dossier: ~400-500 L feedwater with 50-70% RO reject depending on plant and operating mode (EcoDialysis_Perplexity.pdf; Water Sustainability Practices in Dialysis_OpenAI.pdf; Clinical and Technical Advancements in Dialysis_Gemini.pdf).</li>
<li>Reference-center baseline model: 100 patients x 3 sessions/week x 156 sessions/year = 15,600 sessions/year.</li>
<li>At 270 L RO reject/session, annual reusable stream is ~4,200 m3/year before capture losses (model assumption derived from deep-research ranges).</li>
<li>Named proven cases now: Canterbury 800 L/h and ~GBP 7,500/year savings; Bristol 13,140 m3/year and >GBP 15,000/year savings; Ashford low-cost design-in (~GBP 2,500) (Water Sustainability Practices in Dialysis_OpenAI.pdf).</li>
<li>Operational optimization proof now: ANZSN disinfection protocol saved >160,000 L water/year, 6.4 MWh/year, 720 L citric-acid solution, and ~$2,400 product cost (Water Sustainability Practices in Dialysis_OpenAI.pdf; Clinical and Technical Advancements in Dialysis_Gemini.pdf).</li>
<li>Equipment modernization proof now: modern Australian RO comparison 357 L and 3.1 kWh per treatment vs older 548 L and 7.2 kWh (Water Sustainability Practices in Dialysis_OpenAI.pdf).</li>
<li>Network-level benchmark: Spain 20-center study mean 476.5 L/session; daily-opening centers 430.20 L/HD and 10.82 kWh/HD vs three-times-weekly centers 629.81 L/HD and 16.79 kWh/HD (Water Sustainability Practices in Dialysis_OpenAI.pdf; Clinical and Technical Advancements in Dialysis_Gemini.pdf).</li>
<li>Regulatory opening exists (EICH, July 2024 decree) but project approval in sensitive health facilities remains site-specific with prefecture/ARS logic and strict sanitary constraints (see Section B).</li>
<li><strong>EcoDialysis deployment boundary:</strong> current deployment is non-clinical reuse only; no return to dialysis-water production and no patient-contact pathway reuse.</li>
</ul>

<h2>B. French Regulation Review: Current State and Forecast</h2>
<div class="card">
<h3>Confirmed legal position (official sources)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Decree <code>n°2024-796</code> (12 July 2024; in force from 1 Sept 2024) establishes CSP framework for domestic uses of waters unfit for human consumption (EICH) in buildings and ERP, including sensitive establishments.</li>
<li>Health facilities are sensitive establishments under this framework.</li>
<li>Sensitive-establishment systems generally require prefectural authorization before first operation, with ARS technical-health input, except narrow derogation cases defined by decree.</li>
<li>Required controls include network separation, anti-backflow protection, signage, preventive risk management, monitoring, and sanitary logbook (<code>carnet sanitaire</code>).</li>
<li>Framework limits use to non-potable domestic uses under defined conditions; it does not authorize using reused water as potable or patient-contact clinical feedwater.</li>
</ul>
<p class="small">Official legal references used: decree text and ministry explanatory pages captured during extraction; detailed citations listed in bibliography [R1]-[R5].</p>
</div>
<div class="grid">
<div class="card warn">
<h3>Likely interpretation for dialysis RO reject projects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Most realistic first use cases: toilets, some cleaning and selected technical uses with dual network and dedicated controls.</li>
<li>Authorization package should include quality characterization, risk analysis, maintenance protocol, and emergency shutoff mode.</li>
<li>Hospital hygiene committee and technical directorate alignment is a practical prerequisite before prefectural filing.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="card risk">
<h3>Site-specific ARS/legal validation required</h3>
<ul>
<li>Exact admissibility of specific uses (laundry, internal cleaning, cooling towers, technical loops) depends on local interpretation and water-quality dossier.</li>
<li>Sampling parameters/frequency and operating constraints can vary by project risk profile.</li>
<li>Any project wording must avoid claiming blanket nationwide pre-approval.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<div class="card risk">
<h3>Not allowed / not recommended in current EcoDialysis deployment</h3>
<ul>
<li>No reuse in dialysis-water production.</li>
<li>No reuse in any pathway that can contact patients directly.</li>
<li>No claim of verified CNAM reimbursement for this infrastructure model unless explicit source evidence is produced for that exact reimbursement route.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<tr><td>Overclaiming ESG/carbon benefit</td><td>Credibility/legal risk.</td><td>Water-only carbon estimate can be overinterpreted.</td><td>Claim-control template with assumptions disclosure.</td><td>Communications + legal</td></tr>
<tr><td>Patient-safety perception risk</td><td>Adoption resistance.</td><td>Perception can block even non-clinical projects.</td><td>Explicit boundary messaging and governance transparency.</td><td>Medical lead</td></tr>
<tr><td>Responsibility split (dialysis vs technical services)</td><td>Operational failures.</td><td>Case studies depend on clear ownership.</td><td>RACI matrix and service-level maintenance contract.</td><td>Hospital COO</td></tr>
</table>

<h2>L. Implementation Blueprint</h2>

<li>Baseline metering: feed, reject, and candidate non-clinical demand nodes.</li>

<li>Demand mapping: toilets, cleaning, irrigation, technical loops by time profile.</li>
<li>Reuse-option ranking: prioritize high-capture, low-risk end uses.</li>
<li>ARS/legal pre-check and dossier strategy (declaration/authorization logic).</li>
<li>Engineering design: dual network, anti-backflow, controls, maintenance access.</li>
<li>Hygiene committee validation and sanitary monitoring plan.</li>
<li>Procurement and contracting with clear performance targets.</li>
<li>Commissioning with acceptance tests and fail-safe validation.</li>
<li>Monitoring and monthly KPI verification.</li>
<li>Annual review, re-audit, and label status update/renewal gate.</li>
</ol>

<h3>M4. Core formulas</h3>
<div class="card">
<ul>
<li><code>Annual sessions = patients x sessions per patient per year</code></li>
<li><code>Potential stream (m3/yr) = annual sessions x reject L/session / 1000</code></li>
<li><code>Reused volume = potential stream x capture rate</code></li>
<li><code>Annual savings (EUR) = reused volume x avoided tariff (EUR/m3)</code></li>
<li><code>CO2e (t/yr) = reused volume x factor (kg/m3) / 1000</code></li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3>M9. Claims allowed vs claims not allowed</h3>
<div class="grid">
